PSMS Architecture Principles

Purpose

The Pathways Success Management System (PSMS) is a participant success management platform designed for the CaPS pathways ecosystem.

The system exists to help staff understand participant engagement, support, progress, development, and intervention needs.

---

Guiding Principle

Participants are the center of the system.

All functionality exists to support participant success.

---

Five Pillars Framework

Engagement

Attendance, seminars, workshops, retreats, cohort meetings.

Support

High Touch Mentor meetings, Well-Being Advisor meetings, MedOpp advising, CASE support.

Progress

GPA, Science GPA, Math/Science GPA, requirements.

Development

Shadowing, clinical experiences, research, service, leadership, professional development.

Intervention

Alerts, follow-ups, resolution tracking.

---

Data Philosophy

Store detailed records.

Display actionable summaries.

Examples:

- Attendance is stored at the event level and summarized by semester.
- Meetings are stored individually and summarized by participant.
- Experiences are summarized by semester with supporting documentation.

---

Scope Protection

PSMS is not:

- an admissions system
- an application tracking system
- an interview tracking system
- a CRM
- a learning management system
- a registrar system

---

Feature Governance Rule

Every proposed feature must answer:

"Which pillar does this support?"

If it does not support Engagement, Support, Progress, Development, or Intervention, it does not belong in PSMS Version 1.
